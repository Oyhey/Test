Practices that can make passwords vulnerable

Having nonunique passwords
Using the same password for multiple accounts makes it incredibly simple for hackers to get access into all of your accounts. The hacker who gains access to a single user account will have access to all of that user's accounts.

Creating obvious passwords
Easy to guess passwords make it easy for hackers to get access to your account. Passwords like “1234” or “password” are very common.

Best practices for password security

Change passwords frequently
This is one of the simpler ways to increase your password security. The most ideal schedule would be to just change your password around once a month, although this can get tedious.

Safely store passwords
Password managers can create and store complex, hard to guess passwords that you can use whenever you need to access your account.

Salting and encrypting
Encrypting your password using a cypher is also a very secure way to strengthen your password. You can also replace letters with numbers. You can also use personal information to make it harder for hackers to guess your password, although if they know your personal information, it makes it easier.
